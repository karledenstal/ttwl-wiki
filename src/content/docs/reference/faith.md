title: Faith
description: Faith and belief in Pike Creek
---
For most residents, faith exists comfortably alongside folklore. People say grace before meals, hang rowan above their doorways, attend Sunday service, and avoid certain roads after dark. Few see any contradiction in these practices.

---
## The Churches

The churches serve as places of worship, community gathering, charity, and support.

Most clergy have no knowledge of the supernatural. Strange events are usually explained through faith, coincidence, human nature, or simple mystery.

Some notice unusual patterns.

Most accept that Pike Creek has always been a strange place.

---
## Faith & Folklore

Religious belief and local superstition often overlap in Pike Creek. Rowan branches hang above doorways, blessed water is kept in homes, protective charms are passed between generations, and old warnings are repeated without anyone remembering exactly where they came from. Many of these traditions have existed for so long that few residents can say whether they began as acts of faith, local folklore, or something else entirely.

---
## The Supernatural

Supernatural beings hold many different views on faith. Some attend church regularly, some avoid it entirely, and others find comfort in religion without fully embracing it. No single faith explains the supernatural world, nor does the supernatural offer proof of any particular belief. In Pike Creek, faith and uncertainty exist side by side, with neither ever fully answering the other.
