title: Secrets
description: The open secret of Pike Creek
---
Pike Creek does not keep its secrets particularly well.

The problem is not that the truth is hidden.

The problem is that it is scattered.

Generations of residents have documented strange events, unexplained sightings, local folklore, and family traditions. Most of it survives in journals, church records, newspaper clippings, archived reports, and stories passed between generations.

Individually, these accounts seem insignificant.

Together, they paint a very different picture.

---
## The Argyris Library

The Pike Creek Public Library is maintained by the Argyris family, whose connection to the town stretches back to its earliest days.

Over generations, they have preserved journals, photographs, newspapers, maps, oral histories, and personal accounts that might otherwise have been lost. As a result, the library's archives are unusually complete for a town of Pike Creek's size.

Researchers often find records stretching back farther than expected, documenting the same locations, families, and strange events across multiple generations. Most visitors see a well-maintained local history collection. Those who spend enough time in the archives occasionally notice how often the same stories seem to repeat.

---
## City Hall

Property records, historical surveys, maintenance reports, and old maps reveal a town shaped by events that were rarely explained properly.

Buildings were abandoned without clear reason. Roads were rerouted. Construction projects quietly disappeared. Entire areas were marked unsafe despite no obvious hazard.

The records exist.

The explanations rarely do.

---
## Church Records

For more than a century, Pike Creek's churches have documented births, deaths, marriages, illnesses, disasters, and community events.

Among otherwise ordinary records are occasional entries that stand out: descriptions of impossible recoveries, warnings about particular places, and references to events that seem difficult to explain. Most were written by people attempting to understand what they witnessed using the language available to them.

---
## Family Histories

Many of Pike Creek's oldest families possess journals, letters, photographs, and stories passed down through generations.

Most are treated as family curiosities. Yet some contain observations that match accounts recorded elsewhere in town decades apart.

No single family possesses the whole story.

Many possess small pieces of it.

---
## Folk Remedies & Superstitions

Pike Creek is full of traditions nobody can fully explain. Rowan hangs above doorways. Blessed water sits in pantries. Certain roads are avoided after dark, and certain whistles are never used in the woods.

Most residents follow these customs because their parents and grandparents did before them. Few stop to ask where they originated.

Fewer still ask why some of them seem to work.

---
## The Truth

The truth of Pike Creek is not buried beneath the town.

It exists in plain sight—in books, records, family stories, and traditions.

Most people simply never have reason to connect the pieces.

Those who do often discover that Pike Creek has been trying to explain itself for generations.
