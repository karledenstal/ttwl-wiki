---
title: Hollowborn
description: Some people survive encounters with the supernatural. Others die.
---
A Hollowborn is someone who died at the hands of the supernatural and was brought back through the **Rite of Hollowing**, an ancient ritual performed by another Hollowborn.

The Rite can only be performed on those killed by supernatural forces — creatures, curses, rituals, or other unnatural influences. Ordinary deaths remain beyond its reach, though nobody truly understands why.

What returns is alive.

But changed.

Every Hollowborn bears the mark of their death. Some carry scars that never heal. Others bear stranger reminders. All remember dying.

The Rite grants a second life, but not a perfect one.

---
## A Life After Death

Hollowborn experience the world differently than ordinary people.

Food loses much of its flavor. Sleep is often restless. Emotions can feel muted, distant, or difficult to access. Many describe life as though a thin layer of glass separates them from everyone else.

They are still capable of joy, love, grief, and friendship.

Those feelings simply do not come as easily as they once did.

Some adapt quickly.

Others spend decades trying to feel alive again.

---
## The Second Life

Every Hollowborn was once an ordinary person with a life, family, ambitions, regrets, and unfinished business.

Some were brought back because they were loved.

Some because they were needed.

Some because another Hollowborn simply could not leave them dead.

Whatever the reason, they all share the same truth:

They have already died once.

---
## Scholars of the Unseen

Over generations, Hollowborn became keepers of supernatural knowledge.

They document creatures, rituals, remedies, and strange phenomena most people dismiss as folklore. Many maintain journals, archives, and personal collections built over decades.

While some learn to fight, experience is usually their greatest weapon.

When confronted with the unknown, a Hollowborn is often more interested in understanding it than destroying it.

---
## The Hollowborn Way

Most Hollowborn value knowledge over courage.

They favor observation, containment, and understanding before direct confrontation. Many threats can be avoided or controlled long before violence becomes necessary.

They are capable fighters when required, but most view violence as a failure of preparation rather than a solution.

A Hollowborn who draws a weapon has often exhausted every other option first.

---
## Gifts of the Hollow

The Rite changes everyone differently.

Every Hollowborn develops unusual abilities tied to life, death, and the ley lines. Some sense supernatural influences, perceive ley lines directly, detect rituals, or uncover traces left behind by powerful events.

No two Hollowborn are exactly alike.

Their gifts tend to reveal, understand, or endure rather than dominate.

---
## The Cost

The Rite was never meant to restore the dead.

For years, even decades, a Hollowborn may appear unchanged. Eventually, however, the resurrection begins to fail. Bodies weaken. Recovery slows. Age returns where none existed before.

This deterioration is known as **the Hollowing**.

Every Hollowborn knows their second life is temporary.

Death was delayed.

Not defeated.

---
## Pain & Death

Hollowborn still feel pain.

Injuries hurt, exhaustion lingers, and illness remains unpleasant. Many simply experience pain as muted or distant, allowing them to endure wounds that would incapacitate most people.

Death, however, is complicated.

Hollowborn are extraordinarily difficult to kill through ordinary means. Injuries that would kill a human may instead leave them motionless for minutes, hours, or days before they awaken again.

The injury remains.

The recovery remains.

The scar often remains.

Death is not erased — only survived.

Supernatural forces are different. A Hollowborn owes their existence to the supernatural, and it is the supernatural that can end it. When killed by a supernatural creature, phenomenon, or force, there is no return.

The second chance has been spent.

---
## Children & Legacy

Hollowborn cannot have children.

No confirmed Hollowborn pregnancy has ever been recorded, and no one knows why.

Instead, they leave their mark through journals, teachings, mentorship, and the knowledge they pass on to future generations.

---
## Themes

Hollowborn stories often explore:
- Life after death
- Duty and responsibility
- Knowledge and preservation
- Isolation and connection
- The cost of a second chance
- What it means to remain human after losing part of yourself

A Hollowborn is not someone who escaped death.

They are someone living on borrowed time, trying to decide what to do with it.
