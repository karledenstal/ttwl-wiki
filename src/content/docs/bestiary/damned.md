---
title: Damned
description: The Damned are beings caught between hunger and regret.
---
They were once human, but something in death prevented them from moving on. They return with their memories, personality, and sense of self intact, yet changed by an endless hunger that must be fed.

To outsiders they are often mistaken for demons.

Most Damned know the truth.

A demon is something foreign.

A Damned is still the same person.

And that is the problem.

---
## The Hunger

Unlike the Faded, who feed on blood, the Damned feed on emotions and vitality: joy, hope, love, ambition, fear, grief, and countless others.

Victims rarely suffer permanent harm, but repeated feeding can leave them emotionally exhausted, apathetic, and hollow.

Most Damned learn moderation. Some rely on willing donors. Others maintain long-term arrangements, allowing victims time to recover. Some simply ignore the consequences.

No matter the method, the hunger never goes away.

---
## The Balance

Every Damned walks a narrow line.

Too little sustenance causes them to weaken, lose their connection to reality, and eventually become banished.

Too much causes their humanity to erode as the hunger takes over.

Most spend their lives balancing survival against self-control.

Fed enough to exist.

Restrained enough to remain themselves.

---
## The Void Form

Most Damned appear human, though subtle signs often reveal something is wrong: unnatural eyes, strange markings, lingering smiles, or shadows that behave incorrectly.

As they feed, these traits become harder to conceal.

Eventually their human appearance fractures into a unique manifestation known as a **Void Form**.

No two Void Forms are alike. Some develop elongated limbs or impossible jaws. Others become cloaked in living darkness, exposed bone, or shifting shapes that defy reason.

The stronger a Damned becomes, the harder it is to hide what they truly are.

---
## Banishment

The Damned cannot be permanently killed, only banished.

When sufficiently harmed, they disappear from the world entirely.

What happens afterward is unknown.

Hollowborn often call this absence **the Void**, though whether it is a place, a state of being, or something else remains uncertain. Returned Damned describe vastly different experiences: isolation, old memories, or nothing at all.

One detail is consistent:

No Damned wishes to experience it twice.

---
## Power

Among supernatural beings, the Damned are considered the most powerful.

They possess immense strength, resilience, and the ability to manipulate the energies they consume. Some command darkness, suppress sound, drain warmth, or distort perception itself.

Yet every gift is tied to hunger.

Every advantage carries a cost.

And losing control can be just as dangerous as starvation.

---
## Regret & Forgiveness

The Damned are not defined by evil.

Many were selfish. Many made terrible choices. Many hurt people they loved.

What defines them is regret.

Every Damned carries a wound they cannot release: a failure, betrayal, mistake, or loss they cannot forgive themselves for. The hunger feeds on that wound as much as any emotion.

Rarely, a Damned moves on.

Not through punishment.

Not through redemption.

But through forgiveness.

Those who achieve it simply disappear.

Whether they find peace or cease to exist is unknown.

---
## Children & Endings

The Damned cannot create new life. No confirmed pregnancy involving a Damned has ever succeeded, and no one knows why.

For some, this is merely part of their condition.

For others, it is a painful reminder that the future they imagined is gone.

Yet unlike many supernatural beings, the Damned possess something rare:

An ending.

When they finally make peace with themselves, they disappear from the world forever.

---
## Themes

Damned stories often explore:
- Regret and self-forgiveness
- Hunger and temptation
- Power and restraint
- Identity after death
- Guilt, shame, and self-deception
- Learning to live with your mistakes

A Damned is not haunted by a monster.

They are haunted by themselves.
