---
title: Lycanthrope
description: Lycanthropes are people who share their existence with a wolf.
---
Often called werewolves, they live between two sets of instincts: the human mind and the wolf within. Neither is separate from the other. They share the same body, memories, and ultimately the same life.

The wolf is not a curse or a possession.

It is part of them.

Learning to coexist with it is the defining challenge of every Lycan's life.

---
## The Wolf

Every Lycan carries a wolf within them.

The wolf possesses its own instincts, desires, fears, and priorities. It values survival above all else: hunger, territory, safety, family, and self-preservation.

Young Lycans often view the wolf as an enemy to suppress. Older Lycans usually reach a different conclusion.

The wolf is not something to defeat.

It is something to understand.

Balance rarely comes easily.

For most, it takes years.

---
## Transformation

A Lycan's wolf form is exactly that: a wolf.

Not a man-wolf.

Not a monster on two legs.

A wolf.

Large, powerful, and unmistakably unnatural, but still recognizably canine.

Transformation is violent and painful. Bones break, muscles tear, and the body reshapes itself through an exhausting process few Lycans ever describe as pleasant.

The first transformation leaves a permanent scar.

Every Lycan carries a reminder of the moment their life changed forever.

---
## The Full Moon

The full moon always triggers a transformation.

No amount of discipline or experience can prevent it.

Extreme trauma, life-threatening injury, suicide attempts, or situations where the wolf believes survival is at risk may also force an involuntary shift.

The wolf does not want to die.

When survival is threatened, it may take control.

---
## Balance

A Lycan who constantly suppresses the wolf often becomes restless, anxious, irritable, and unable to sleep.

A Lycan who gives themselves entirely to the wolf risks losing touch with their humanity.

Neither extreme is healthy.

The healthiest relationship is rarely one of control.

It is one of coexistence.

Young Lycans often experience transformation as a loss of control, with the wolf's instincts overwhelming their own. With time and trust, the relationship can become a partnership. Human and wolf remain distinct, but neither exists without the other.

---
## Instinct & Survival

Even in human form, Lycans possess enhanced strength, reflexes, stamina, senses, and resilience.

Their appetites tend to be larger than those of ordinary humans, and many develop a preference for rare or raw meat.

The wolf's instincts never fully disappear. A Lycan may sense danger before understanding why, trust someone immediately, or feel uneasy around them without explanation.

These instincts are not always correct.

But they are rarely random.

In wolf form, Lycans become intelligent but instinct-driven predators. They excel as hunters, particularly in forests and wilderness, where they rely on patience, tracking, and positioning rather than direct confrontation.

Many supernatural creatures are stronger.

Few are better hunters.

Yet they remain animals.

A wolf values survival more than victory.

When faced with overwhelming odds, most Lycans retreat rather than die.

---
## Recovery

Transformation places enormous strain on the body.

After returning to human form, Lycans are often exhausted, dehydrated, disoriented, and vulnerable. Recovery may take a full day or longer.

While transformation can mend many injuries sustained in wolf form, the aftermath leaves Lycans at their weakest.

Experienced Lycans learn to prepare for this whenever possible.

---
## Aconite

Aconite has long been associated with Lycanthropy.

Though it cannot cure or remove the wolf, properly prepared aconite remedies can reduce anxiety, agitation, and the growing unease many Lycans experience before a full moon.

The wolf instinctively dislikes aconite, often reacting with discomfort or dread.

Its effects are temporary, and repeated use becomes less effective over time.

For most Lycans, it is a tool for management rather than a solution.

---
## Canis Hysteria

Lycanthropy is spread through a supernatural condition known as **Canis Hysteria**, most commonly transmitted through bites or deep scratches.

Most victims never survive the process. Those who do eventually develop a wolf of their own.

Lycanthropy cannot be inherited. Although Lycans remain physically capable of reproduction, no pregnancy has ever survived the transformation cycle.

As a result, every Lycan was once something else before becoming what they are now.

The condition cannot be cured.

Only endured.

For some, it feels like a loss.

For others, it feels like coming home.

---
## Weaknesses

Despite their resilience, Lycans are far from invulnerable.

Silver remains one of the few materials capable of reliably harming them. Small amounts are often little more than an irritation, but concentrated exposure can be devastating.

Silver wounds heal slowly, and silver ammunition is widely regarded as one of the most effective ways to stop a Lycan.

Like the wolf itself, a Lycan's greatest strength is survival—not invincibility.

---
## Themes

Lycan stories often explore:
- Instinct and reason
- Control and coexistence
- Identity and self-acceptance
- Survival and responsibility
- Trusting yourself
- Learning to live with what cannot be removed

A Lycan is not learning how to control the wolf.

They are learning how to live with it.
